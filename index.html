Radiation Basics

What is Radiation?

Radiation is energy emitted as particles or electromagnetic waves. It‚Äôs categorized as non‚Äëionizing (radio, microwave, visible light) and ionizing (X‚Äërays, gamma, alpha, beta, neutrons) that can remove electrons from atoms.

Units & Measurement

  • Activity (Becquerel, Bq): decays per second.

Health & Protection

Deterministic effects (burns, radiation sickness) occur at high doses; stochastic effects (cancer risk) increase with dose probability. Protection uses the ALARA principle: Time, Distance, Shielding.

Nuclear Power Plants

How They Work (High‚ÄëLevel)

Controlled fission in a reactor core releases heat. A coolant takes this heat to produce steam that spins turbines to make electricity. Safety systems include control rods, negative reactivity feedbacks, multiple barriers, and robust containment.

  • PWR: water under high pressure; separate steam generator loop.
  • BWR: water boils in the core; steam goes directly to the turbine.
  • PHWR/CANDU: heavy‚Äëwater moderator; natural uranium fuel; on‚Äëpower refueling.
  • Fast Breeder: fast neutrons; can breed more fuel than consumed (e.g., from U‚Äë238 to Pu‚Äë239).

Safety Snapshot

Modern plants emphasize defense‚Äëin‚Äëdepth, redundancy, passive cooling, emergency planning, and strict regulation. Workers use dosimetry and controlled access; public doses are kept far below regulatory limits.

The Atomic Era (High‚ÄëLevel Overview)

Fission & Fusion Weapons (Conceptual Only)

Fission weapons release energy by rapidly splitting heavy nuclei (e.g., U‚Äë235, Pu‚Äë239). Fusion weapons use extreme conditions to fuse light nuclei (e.g., deuterium, tritium). This site does not provide construction details and condemns harmful use.

The Demon Core (1945‚Äì46)

A plutonium core used for experiments at Los Alamos caused two fatal criticality accidents due to unsafe manual procedures. Lesson: strict protocols, remote handling, and engineered interlocks save lives.

FAQ & Quick Facts

Is all radiation dangerous?

No. We live with natural background radiation. Risk depends on type, energy, dose, and exposure time. Ionizing radiation at high doses is dangerous; regulated uses manage dose limits to keep exposure low.

Can nuclear plants ever be 100% safe?

No complex system is zero‚Äërisk. The aim is as low as reasonably achievable risk via layers of engineered safety and strong safety culture.

Will you provide instructions to build bombs or reactors?

No. This site is for learning and public safety awareness. It avoids operational details that could be misused.

What's the difference between radiation and radioactivity?

Radioactivity is the property of unstable atoms to emit radiation. Radiation is the energy/particles emitted during radioactive decay.
